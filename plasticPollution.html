Plastic Pollution

99% of our sea animals are being hurt.

What is it?

Drowning in plastic.

The boom of plastic began around WWII, years later those plastics could still be found on our planet. Cheap, easy, and convienent, plastic has overtaken out lives. Humans a are highly dependent on this material but rarely know the consequences of these products. Because plastic has been around for 70 years, we don't know how long it takes for the material to decompose. However, it is estimated that one plastic bottle takes 450 years to decompose and one plastic bag takes 500 years. Where are we putting all this plastic that still has a reusable life? Most of it ends up in our oceans, approximately 9 million tons. Because of this, ocean ife is suffering. Sea turtles, whales, fish, and many more are injesting microplastics which can destroy their systems and lead to death. Approximately, 700 species have injested some sort of plastic.
