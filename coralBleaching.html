Coral Bleaching

When water turns too warm.

What is it?

Healthy reefs = healthy ocean = healthy planet.

Coral reefs make up only about 1% of the ocean floor, but they should not be underestimated. The reefs shelter 25% of known ocean species, protect shorelines, support fishing industries, and provides a backbone for the economy. The biggest threat to the reefs is climate change and warming waters. Because of warming waters and their limited temperature range in which they can live, corals eject their algaes (zooxanthalae) which produce their beautiful and vibrant colors. Additionally, urban and agricultural runoff can also harm reefs when they carry pollutants and pollute ocean water. Just like humans, once corals are bleached, they are under so much stress that they become susceptible to many diseases which can result to death.

Count the Numbers
